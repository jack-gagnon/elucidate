Human Networks - Trust, Communication, Efficiency

Network visualizations can illuminate the behavior of complex self-organizing systems.

As an example - The image adjacent is a fictional 1000 node network in phase space. The color of each node is determined by the number of connections to others in the sample space.

This simple model does not account for all the variables that complicate interactions; however, the picture does highlight the complexity that arises within a confined ecosystem. Varying, individual, limitations on capacity and bandwidth automatically create unit-clusters.

Creating space in time, and fostering more efficient interactions.

Behind an anthropologic lens - trust and cooperation require effective communication; A clear outline of goals, objectives, and intentions for participants.

The diagram below illustrates how preferred methods of communication can scew the quality of interactions between members, and thus the efficiency of the total set.

When you visualize groups in this way, clusters emerge; the equilibrium formation is dependent on a variety of factors (tenure, physical location, and predispositions - to list a few). Context, combined with this unique procedure, could lead to a more efficient organization of these 12 team members. By paying closer attention to manageable behaviors - this fictional form-group could create a much more symetric shape; eliminating information silos, and hopefully leading to observable efficiencies through sub-group formation and delegation.

The visuals, matched to internal performance indicators, could provide value to organizations undergoing agile (, sometimes turbulent,) digital transformations.
